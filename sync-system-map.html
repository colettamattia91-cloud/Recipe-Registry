Recipe Registry / Sync Model

System map of the guild sync pipeline

This page models the live synchronization design now implemented in Recipe Registry: peer discovery through HELLO, version and build-channel gating, opportunistic manifest exchange through MREQ / MANI, direct recipe fetches through REQ / SNAP, and the recovery paths that keep partial transfers from turning into storms.

Discovery Peers surface via HELLO and enter runtime only after channel and protocol checks.
Knowledge MANI batches advertise block fingerprints before expensive snapshot transfer begins.
Transfer REQ opens a direct session; SNAP chunks stream only the requested owner or replica data.
Recovery Missing MANI seqs and stalled SNAP sessions recover locally instead of punishing healthy peers.

    Version Gate

    buildChannel, wireVersion, and capabilities decide eligibility before a peer can affect queues.

    Recovery

    Partial MANI and SNAP sessions recover through targeted resend and member retry, not peer-wide exile.

    Manual Force

    Operator escape hatches remain available for `/rr manifest`, post-wipe rebuilds, and true missing-cache fallback.

    The key idea in the current solution is that addonVersion does not control sync compatibility. Compatibility is driven by buildChannel + wireVersion + capabilities, while automatic MANI traffic is kept idempotent and slow replica transfers no longer degrade the whole peer through aggressive backoff or fake session deadlines.
